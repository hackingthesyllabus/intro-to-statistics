Numeric outcome

Use a t procedure.

Before you pick a formula

1
Estimate or test?
Estimating uses a confidence interval. Testing uses a hypothesis test.
2
What kind of outcome?
Numeric outcomes use t. Categorical outcomes use z or chi square.
3
One sample or two?
One sample compares to a known value. Two sample compares two groups.
4
Direction?
Different means two tailed. Greater than or less than means one tailed.

Confidence intervals

CI

Confidence interval for a mean

Numeric outcome. Estimate a population mean.

\[ar{Y} \pm t^* s_{ar{Y}}\]
\[s_{ar{Y}} = rac{s_Y}{\sqrt{N}}, \quad df = N - 1\]
CI

Confidence interval for a proportion

Categorical outcome. Estimate a population proportion.

\[\hat{p} \pm z^* \sqrt{ rac{\hat{p}(1-\hat{p})}{n}}\]

t tests

t

One sample t test

One sample mean compared to a known or hypothesized population mean.

\[t = rac{ar{Y} - \mu_0}{s_{ar{Y}}}\]
\[s_{ar{Y}} = rac{s_Y}{\sqrt{N}}, \quad df = N - 1\]
t

Two sample t test

Compare the means of two independent groups.

\[t = rac{ar{Y}_1 - ar{Y}_2}{s_{ar{Y}_1 - ar{Y}_2}}\]
\[s_{ar{Y}_1 - ar{Y}_2} = \sqrt{\left(s_{ar{Y}_1} ight)^2 + \left(s_{ar{Y}_2} ight)^2}\]

z tests

z

One proportion z test

One sample proportion compared to a known or hypothesized population proportion.

\[z = rac{\hat{p} - \pi_0}{s_{\pi_0}}\]
\[s_{\pi_0} = \sqrt{ rac{\pi_0(1-\pi_0)}{n}}\]
z

Two proportion z test

Compare proportions of two independent groups.

\[z = rac{\hat{p}_1 - \hat{p}_2}{SE_{\hat{p}_1-\hat{p}_2}}\]
\[SE_{\hat{p}_1-\hat{p}_2} = \sqrt{\hat{p}(1-\hat{p})\left( rac{1}{n_1}+ rac{1}{n_2} ight)}\]

Chi square

χ²

Chi square test of association

Both variables are categorical and the data are given in a contingency table.

\[\chi^2 = \sum rac{(f_o-f_e)^2}{f_e}\]
\[f_e = rac{( ext{column total})( ext{row total})}{n}, \quad df = (r-1)(c-1)\]
